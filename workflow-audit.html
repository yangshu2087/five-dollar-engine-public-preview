AI workflow audit: 7 operating questions before automation

Use this lightweight audit before letting AI touch a recurring workflow, a customer-facing deliverable, or any action with account, payment, or publishing risk.

The seven questions

  1. Outcome: what exact user, revenue, quality, or time metric should change?
  2. Context: what files, examples, screenshots, account state, or external facts does the AI need?
  3. Risk: what action would be irreversible, public, expensive, sensitive, or hard to roll back?
  4. Approval: what exact sentence or reviewed artifact unlocks that risky action?
  5. Smallest safe test: what can be run locally, read-only, or with a single bounded manual action?
  6. Evidence: what screenshot, command output, permalink, ledger row, or browser check proves the result?
  7. Compounding asset: what template, checklist, script, memory note, or product asset should survive this run?

How to use it

Before work starts

Write the goal, non-goals, done criteria, and verification command before editing or publishing anything.

Before external action

Separate local preparation from external transmission: posting, sending, deploying, checkout changes, and ad spend all need explicit approval.

After evidence arrives

Record only observed counts and reviewed artifacts. Do not infer clicks, replies, sales, or revenue from a green local report.

Boundary: No checkout, email capture, ad spend, or income result is promised.
